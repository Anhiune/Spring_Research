Research question
“Does export-control / geopolitical-risk sentiment increase the risk premium and future volatility for semiconductor stocks, especially for firms with higher China/policy exposure?”

My hypotheses (and the opposite)
H1 (pricing / risk premium channel)
When  spikes, exposed semiconductor stocks will have more negative abnormal returns around policy announcement days (and/or show stronger repricing immediately after).
Opposite / null (H0-1): Policy-risk sentiment is not priced, so it does not explain abnormal returns around events once I control for the market.
H2 (uncertainty / volatility channel)
When policy-risk sentiment increases today, realized volatility will increase over the next 1–5 trading days (and possibly the next 20 days).
Opposite / null (H0-2): Policy-risk sentiment does not predict future volatility beyond what past volatility and market volatility already explain.
H3 (exposure heterogeneity)
The effects in H1 and H2 will be stronger for firms with higher exposure to export controls (China revenue dependence, restricted product lines, or supply-chain position).
Opposite / null (H0-3): The effect is not systematically different by exposure.
